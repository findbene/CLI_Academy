# Summarize findings for humans

## Objective
In this lesson, you will learn how to transform raw data from a table or brief into a concise, human-friendly summary. Your goal is to extract the most important insights and present them in a way that an executive can quickly understand and act upon, while also responsibly noting any important caveats or limitations. This skill is vital for clear communication and effective decision-making!

## Walkthrough

1.  **Understand Your Audience:** Imagine you're explaining complex information to someone who needs the "big picture" quickly. They trust you to highlight what's crucial.
2.  **Identify the Core Message:** Look at the provided table or brief. What are the 2-3 most significant findings, trends, or insights? Don't get lost in the details; focus on the most impactful information.
3.  **Draft Your Summary:** Start by stating your key findings clearly and directly. Use simple language. For example, instead of "Q3 revenues increased by 15% from Q2," you might say, "We saw strong revenue growth in Q3, increasing by 15% compared to the previous quarter."
4.  **Add Important Caveats:** This is a crucial safety step! Think about what *isn't* clear from the data or what might influence the findings. Are there any assumptions you made? Is the data for a specific period only? For instance, you might add, "Please note these figures are preliminary and may be subject to minor adjustments." or "This analysis focuses solely on domestic sales, and international trends may differ." Providing caveats prevents misunderstandings and ensures your audience has a complete, accurate context.
5.  **Review for Clarity and Conciseness:** Read your summary aloud. Does it flow well? Is every sentence necessary? Remove jargon and redundant words. Aim for a summary that can be read and understood in under a minute. Remember, clear communication is always a safe and effective approach.
