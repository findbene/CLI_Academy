# Generate a research brief

## Objective

By the end of this lesson, you will be able to leverage CLI AI tools to transform raw research inputs into a structured research brief. Your brief will feature clear sections, explicit statements of uncertainty, open questions for further investigation, and concise evidence notes, all generated efficiently from the command line.

## Walkthrough

1.  **Prepare Your Research Inputs:** First, gather the information you want to turn into a brief. For this exercise, you can imagine some research notes or even type out a few sentences about a hypothetical topic. The goal is to feed this raw information to our AI tool.

2.  **Introducing the `llm` Command:** We'll use the `llm` command, which is a powerful and safe way to interact with AI models directly from your terminal. It takes your text input, processes it, and outputs text. Crucially, `llm` *does not* modify any files on your system unless you specifically redirect its output (e.g., `llm "..." > brief.md`). You're just asking it to generate text.

3.  **Crafting Your Brief Request:** The magic happens when you tell `llm` exactly what kind of brief you need. We'll provide clear instructions to ensure the output includes sections, open questions, and notes.
    Type the following command into your terminal. When you see `[PASTE YOUR RESEARCH NOTES HERE]`, replace it with your actual notes (you can type a few sentences or paste a larger block of text).

    ```bash
    llm "Based on the following research notes, please generate a comprehensive research brief.
    Your brief should be structured with the following explicit sections:
    1.  **Summary:** A concise overview of the key points.
    2.  **Key Findings & Evidence:** Detail the main discoveries, including specific notes or phrases from the input as evidence.
    3.  **Explicit Uncertainties & Open Questions:** Clearly state what information is missing, unclear, or requires further investigation. Frame these as specific questions.
    4.  **Next Steps:** Suggest actionable questions or areas for future research.

    ---
    [PASTE YOUR RESEARCH NOTES HERE, e.g., 'The new project launch showed a 15% increase in user engagement during the first week. However, retention rates for new users are still below target, only 30% after one month. We suspect onboarding friction.']
    ---"
    ```

4.  **Review the Output:** After `llm` processes your request, it will display the generated brief directly in your terminal. Take a moment to read through it. Does it have all the sections? Are the uncertainties and open questions clear? This is your brief!
