## What You'll See

By the end of this lesson, you will have a practical framework for categorizing CoWork outputs by trust level, know what hallucination looks like and how to catch it, understand what data to never put into CoWork, and be able to explain responsibly what CoWork is to colleagues who ask.

## Why This Matters

CoWork is powerful enough that it is tempting to trust its outputs without checking. That trust, when misplaced, creates real problems: an executive proposal citing a study that does not exist, a legal clause generated by an AI that does not reflect actual law, or a financial projection built on a hallucinated market size. This lesson gives you the mental model to avoid those mistakes.

## Core Concepts

### The Three-Category Framework

Every CoWork output falls into one of three categories. Your review requirement changes based on which category it is in.

---

**Category 1: Safe to use after light review**

These outputs involve rearranging, reformatting, or paraphrasing content you provided. CoWork is not generating facts — it is processing your input.

- Reformatting a document you wrote (restructuring, condensing, improving readability)
- Translating a document into another language
- Summarizing text you uploaded (the source is your document, not CoWork's training)
- Creating a table of contents or outline from existing content
- Cleaning up grammar and style in a draft you wrote

**Review needed:** Read for quality and accuracy to your intent. No fact-checking required — the facts are yours.

---

**Category 2: Must verify before use**

These outputs involve CoWork generating content from its training data or making inferences. The content may be plausible but inaccurate.

- Statistics, market sizes, or industry figures CoWork cites
- Citations, studies, or reports CoWork references
- Company-specific facts (funding, headcount, products, pricing)
- Historical events or timeline specifics
- Technical specifications or regulatory requirements
- Anything that reads as a specific fact rather than a general principle

**Review needed:** Verify every specific claim against a primary source before using it. Do not present CoWork-generated statistics in a client meeting without checking them.

---

**Category 3: Do not outsource to CoWork**

These outputs require human expertise, judgment, and accountability that CoWork cannot provide.

- **Legal advice:** Contract clauses, liability questions, compliance guidance. CoWork may produce text that looks like legal language but is not legally sound. Use an attorney.
- **Medical advice:** Diagnoses, treatment recommendations, medication interactions. Do not use CoWork for anything health-related where the stakes are real.
- **Financial advice:** Investment recommendations, tax strategy, accounting judgments. CoWork can help you understand concepts, not make financial decisions.
- **Any high-stakes decision:** Hiring, firing, major business pivots, public statements. CoWork can help you think through a decision; you make the decision.
- **Anything requiring current events knowledge:** CoWork has a training cutoff. It does not know what happened last week.

<WarnBlock>The most dangerous CoWork outputs are ones that look authoritative but are incorrect in ways that are hard to detect — fabricated citations, wrong statistics presented confidently, plausible legal language that does not actually apply. These fall in Category 2 and 3. Always verify before relying on them.</WarnBlock>

### What Hallucination Looks Like

Hallucination in CoWork outputs typically looks like:

**The specific-but-wrong citation:**
> "According to a 2023 McKinsey report on digital transformation, 67% of companies report..."

The statistic sounds precise. The report may not exist, or may say something different. Always search for the actual report before citing it.

**The plausible but invented number:**
> "The B2B SaaS market is expected to reach $1.2 trillion by 2027..."

CoWork generates numbers that sound plausible for the context. They may be completely fabricated. Verify with a primary source.

**The wrong-version technical fact:**
> "In the current version of the EU GDPR, organizations have 72 hours to..."

CoWork's training data may include the regulation, but regulations change. Always verify current requirements from official sources.

**How to catch it:** After getting a CoWork output with specific claims, pick 3-5 of the most important ones and verify them independently. If 2 or more are wrong, treat the whole output as Category 2 — verify everything.

### Your Personal Review Checklist

After every significant CoWork output:

- [ ] Does the output answer the actual question I asked?
- [ ] Are all cited sources, studies, and reports real? (Search for them)
- [ ] Are all specific statistics and numbers verifiable?
- [ ] Is the tone and format right for my audience?
- [ ] Would I be comfortable putting my name on this?
- [ ] Have I checked anything in Category 3 against a domain expert?

### Data Privacy: What Not to Put Into CoWork

CoWork sends your inputs to Anthropic's servers for processing. Before uploading any document or pasting any data, ask yourself:

**Can I share this?**
- Client names, contracts, or communications without permission — **no**
- Employee personal data (salaries, performance reviews, medical) — **no**
- Your company's confidential IP, trade secrets, or unreleased product plans — **check your policy**
- Customer PII (names, emails, addresses) — **only if compliant with your privacy policy and applicable law**
- Anonymized or aggregated data — **generally yes**
- Publicly available information — **yes**

When in doubt, anonymize first. Replace real names with "Client A" and real numbers with representative but not actual figures.

### Research Preview Responsibility

Because CoWork is in research preview, Anthropic is still learning:
- Where it is most reliable and where it fails
- Which use cases it handles well vs. poorly
- What additional guardrails or features users need

If you encounter outputs that are clearly wrong, confusing, or problematic, use the feedback mechanism to report it. Your feedback helps Anthropic improve the product for everyone.

CoWork will change during the research preview. Features that exist today may change tomorrow. Outputs that were reliable last month may behave differently after an update. Treat the product as capable but evolving, not as a stable production tool with guaranteed behavior.

## Key Takeaways

- Category 1 (reformatting, summarizing your own content): light review.
- Category 2 (CoWork-generated facts, citations, statistics): verify all specific claims.
- Category 3 (legal, medical, financial, high-stakes decisions): do not outsource.
- Hallucination looks specific and confident. Verify citations and statistics before using them.
- Never put client PII, confidential IP, or sensitive personal data into CoWork without a clear legal basis.
