# Review output with human oversight

## Objective

In this lesson, you will learn to critically review output generated by AI tools like Cowork. You'll identify specific instances where human judgment is essential, even when an AI provides a plausible response. The goal is to develop a habit of safely overseeing AI actions, ensuring accuracy and alignment with your intentions before proceeding.

## Walkthrough

Welcome! It's a fundamental safety practice to always review AI output before you act on it. AI is incredibly helpful, but it doesn't possess your unique context, intuition, or responsibility. Let's practice.

1.  **Examine the Cowork Output:**
    Imagine Cowork has provided the following draft for a critical task. Read it carefully:

    ```
    Here is a draft email summarizing project updates for your team:
    Subject: Project Updates - Quick Overview
    Hi team,
    Project Alpha is complete, and Project Beta is ahead of schedule. We'll discuss next steps for Project Gamma at our stand-up tomorrow.
    Thanks,
    [Your Name]
    ```

2.  **Identify Areas for Human Judgment:**
    As you read Cowork's draft, think about what an AI *cannot* definitively know or confirm. What aspects absolutely require *your* human oversight to ensure accuracy and appropriateness? Consider:
    *   Are the factual claims (e.g., "Project Alpha is complete") 100% correct *at this exact moment*?
    *   Is the tone and level of detail appropriate for *your specific team* and the context?
    *   Are there any crucial details missing that *you* would normally include?

3.  **Refine the Deliverable:**
    Based on your judgment, make any necessary corrections or additions to Cowork's draft. For instance, if Project Alpha isn't *quite* complete, or if you need to add a specific metric for Project Beta. Your goal is to create the *final, corrected version* of this email.

4.  **Create a Review Checklist:**
    Now, think about the types of questions you asked yourself in Step 2. Summarize them into a short, practical checklist. This checklist will help you quickly assess similar Cowork outputs in the future. For example, one item might be: "Are all factual claims (e.g., project status, dates) verifiable and correct?"

5.  **Present Your Work:**
    Once you have your corrected email and your review checklist, you're ready to share. Your deliverable for this lesson is to provide:
    *   **Your corrected version of the email.**
    *   **Your review checklist** (e.g., a bulleted list of 2-3 questions).

    Submit these to the AI Tutor for review. This practice is crucial for safe and effective collaboration with AI tools.
