# Write the case study and demo script

## Objective

You're ready to transform your capstone project into a compelling narrative! In this lesson, you will craft a concise case study that explains the "why" and "what" of your project. Then, you'll develop a 3-minute demo script that walks an audience through the "how" in an engaging and easy-to-understand way. This deliverable will be a **case study plus a 3-minute demo script**.

## Walkthrough

It's natural to feel a bit daunted by presenting, but remember, you've built something great! Let's break down how to tell its story.

1.  **Revisit Your Capstone's Purpose:** Before writing, take a moment to reflect. What problem were you trying to solve? How did your project help? There are no wrong answers here, just clarity!
2.  **Write Your Case Study (The Story Arc):**
    *   **Identify the Problem:** Start by clearly stating the challenge or pain point your project addresses. *Example: "Users struggle to quickly find available network ports."*
    *   **Describe Your Solution:** Briefly explain how your capstone project provides a unique solution to that problem.
    *   **Highlight the Impact:** What's the benefit? How does your solution make things better, faster, or easier? Keep this section short, focusing on one or two key outcomes.
3.  **Outline Your 3-Minute Demo Script (The Action):**
    *   **The Hook (15-30 seconds):** Start by restating the problem and immediately introduce your project as the solution.
    *   **The "Show" (1.5-2 minutes):** Walk through 2-3 core features or steps that demonstrate your solution in action. Think about the most impactful things you can show. Don't worry about showing *everything*; focus on what best illustrates your case study's points.
    *   **The Wrap-Up (30-45 seconds):** Briefly summarize the main benefit and reiterate how your project solves the initial problem. End with a clear, positive statement.
4.  **Practice and Refine:** Read your script aloud. Does it flow well? Is it genuinely 3 minutes? Don't hesitate to trim jargon or simplify explanations. This isn't about perfection right away, but about clear communication. You've got this!
