# Move between folders the safe way

## Objective
Learn how to safely step into and out of folders using `cd` without getting lost or overwhelmed.

## Walkthrough

Now that you know how to figure out where you are (`pwd` or `cd`) and what's around you (`ls` or `dir`), it's time to actually move!

We use a command called `cd`, which stands for **Change Directory**. 

**Step 1: Stepping "Into" a folder**
If you see a folder when you list the files around you, you can walk inside it.
Type `cd ` (don't forget the space!) followed by the folder's name.

Example: If you see a folder named `Documents`, type:
`cd Documents`
Press Enter. You've now "walked" into the Documents room! Always run your "Where am I?" command here to prove you've moved.

**Step 2: Stepping "Out" of a folder**
What if you walked into the wrong room and want to go back?
To step backwards (out of the current folder and into the one containing it), you use a special shortcut: `..` (two dots).

Type `cd ..`
Press Enter. You have now taken one step backwards!

*Safety tip*: If you ever try to `cd` into a folder that doesn't exist, the terminal will just give you an honest error. It won't break anything.
