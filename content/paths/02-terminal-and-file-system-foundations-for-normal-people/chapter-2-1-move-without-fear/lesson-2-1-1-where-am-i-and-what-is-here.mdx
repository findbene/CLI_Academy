# Where am I and what is here?

## Objective
Learn to confidently figure out exactly where you are in the terminal and see what files are around you, without accidentally changing anything.

## Walkthrough

When you open a terminal, it always drops you into a specific folder on your computer. Before you do *anything*, the safest habit you can build is figuring out exactly where you are. 

Think of it like looking at a map in a mall and finding the "You are here" star. 

**Step 1: Ask "Where am I?"**
Depending on your system, you can use a simple command to print your current location:
- On Mac/Linux (or Git Bash): Type `pwd` (Print Working Directory) and press Enter.
- On Windows (Command Prompt/PowerShell): Type `cd` and press Enter, or use `Get-Location`.

*Safety Tip:* Running `pwd` or `cd` by itself is 100% safe. It only reads information; it doesn't change or move anything.

**Step 2: Ask "What is here?"**
Once you know what folder you are in, you probably want to see what is inside it. 
- On Mac/Linux: Type `ls` (List) and press Enter.
- On Windows: Type `dir` (Directory) and press Enter.

*Safety Tip:* Just like looking around a room, listing files is completely safe. You're just observing.

**Your First Task:**
Open your terminal. Run the command to check your current folder, then run the command to list the files inside it. Copy the output you see, and save it to a file called `directory_map.txt` so your tutor can verify you've mastered observation!
