# Interview Preparation for AI-Adjacent Roles

## What you will build

An `INTERVIEW_PREP.md` file containing 10 common interview questions for AI-adjacent roles and your prepared answers for each. You will draft your initial answers, use Claude to stress-test them (by asking Claude to respond as a skeptical interviewer), and refine until each answer is confident, specific, and under 90 seconds when read aloud.

## Why this matters

AI-adjacent roles are growing faster than the hiring process has adapted to them. Interviewers at these companies often do not have standardized questions — they are figuring out what to ask as the field develops. This means the same question can mean different things at different companies, and a candidate who can bridge technical reality with business impact will stand out.

The ten questions in this lesson are drawn from patterns that repeat across these roles: engineering teams want to know you can build reliably; product teams want to know you can constrain AI behavior; operations teams want to know you can debug what you cannot see; leadership wants to know you have thought about risk. Your answers need to speak to all of these audiences.

The preparation document is also useful during the interview itself. If you have written and rehearsed answers to these ten questions, you are covering roughly 80% of the territory that comes up in AI-adjacent first-round and second-round interviews.

## Before you start

- Complete lessons 27.1.1 and 27.1.2 — your projects and your brief give you the examples for your answers
- Have your CLI Academy project list accessible
- Claude Code running in your terminal
- 30–40 minutes for writing and refinement

## The walkthrough

### Step 1 — Understand the ten questions and what they are really asking

Read these questions and the underlying concern each one probes before writing any answers.

| # | Question | What the interviewer is really asking |
|---|----------|--------------------------------------|
| 1 | "How do you verify that Claude's output is correct?" | Can you build reliable systems, or do you just trust whatever the model produces? |
| 2 | "What safeguards do you put in place for AI systems?" | Do you think about failure modes, or do you build optimistically? |
| 3 | "How do you handle a runaway agent?" | Have you actually dealt with production problems, or just toy demos? |
| 4 | "How would you add AI to our existing workflow?" | Can you assess a real situation and make a proportionate recommendation? |
| 5 | "What do you do when Claude gives a confidently wrong answer?" | Do you have a verification strategy, or do you defer to the model? |
| 6 | "How do you manage AI costs in production?" | Are you thinking like a professional developer or like a hobbyist? |
| 7 | "How do you handle sensitive data when using AI APIs?" | Do you understand data privacy obligations, or are you naive about them? |
| 8 | "What is the biggest limitation of current AI coding tools?" | Are you honest about the technology, or overselling it? |
| 9 | "How would you explain this AI system to a non-technical stakeholder?" | Can you communicate across technical boundaries? |
| 10 | "Where do you see AI tooling in 3 years, and how does that affect your work now?" | Are you building durable skills, or chasing hype? |

### Step 3 — Fill in all ten draft answers

Open `INTERVIEW_PREP.md` and write your draft answers. Allow 2–3 minutes per question. The goal is not perfection — it is getting your real thinking onto the page so Claude can improve it.

Rules for draft answers:
- Use specific project names from your CLI Academy work
- Include at least one concrete number or outcome
- Do not worry about length — you will cut later
- Do not write "I would..." for every answer — you want to describe things you have actually done

### Step 4 — Use Claude to stress-test your answers

Once all ten drafts are written, use Claude as a skeptical interviewer:

**macOS / Linux:**
```bash
claude "Read my INTERVIEW_PREP.md file. For each question, act as a skeptical senior engineer interviewer and give me one follow-up question that would expose a weak or vague answer. Then tell me what specific detail my draft answer is missing that would make it stronger. Do not rewrite my answers — just flag the gaps."
```

**Windows PowerShell:**
```powershell
claude "Read INTERVIEW_PREP.md. For each question, act as a skeptical interviewer and give me one follow-up question that would expose a weak answer. Tell me what specific detail my draft is missing. Do not rewrite my answers — just flag the gaps."
```

Read Claude's feedback. For each question where it identified a gap, revise your draft answer to address that gap, then write the refined version in the `**Refined answer:**` field.

### Step 5 — Tighten each answer to 90 seconds

Read each refined answer aloud and time it. An interview answer should be 60–90 seconds — long enough to be substantive, short enough to leave room for follow-up questions.

If an answer is over 90 seconds, ask Claude to compress it:

```bash
claude "Read my answer to Q3 in INTERVIEW_PREP.md. It is too long. Rewrite it to be 60-90 seconds when read aloud at normal speaking pace, keeping the specific project reference and the concrete example. Do not make it vaguer to make it shorter."
```

### Step 6 — Verify the prep document is complete

**macOS / Linux:**
```bash
# Confirm all 10 questions have refined answers (no placeholder text)
grep -c "Refined answer:" INTERVIEW_PREP.md   # should be 10
```

## What can go wrong

| Problem | Cause | Fix |
|---------|-------|-----|
| Draft answers are generic ("I would make sure the output is correct") with no project references | You skipped pulling specific examples from your CLI Academy projects | Open your project directories and pull one concrete detail per answer before continuing |
| Claude's stress-test identifies the same gap in every answer ("too vague, lacks specifics") | All your draft answers describe what you would do rather than what you have done | Rewrite in past tense: "When I built X, I ran into Y and handled it by Z" |
| Refined answers are longer than the drafts | Claude added context instead of compressing | Explicitly ask Claude: "Rewrite in 60-75 words. Remove any sentence that does not add new information." |
| Q10 answer sounds like a press release about AI | You wrote what you thought you should believe rather than what you actually think | Write your honest uncertainty — "I genuinely do not know when X will be reliable, so I am investing in Y because it matters either way" |
