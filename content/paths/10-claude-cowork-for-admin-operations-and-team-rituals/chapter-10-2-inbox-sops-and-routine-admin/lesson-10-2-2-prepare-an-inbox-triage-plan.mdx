# Prepare an inbox triage plan

## Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to design a personalized inbox triage plan. This plan will include a clear rubric for categorizing emails and a proposed folder or label structure to manage them effectively and safely. Our goal is to create a system where you feel in control, prioritize wisely, and ensure sensitive actions always have human oversight.

## Walkthrough

Let's build a safe and sound system for your inbox, step by step!

1.  **Understand Your Email Flow:** Before organizing, take a moment to consider the types of emails you regularly receive. Are they urgent requests, routine notifications, newsletters, or something else? There's no command here, just a helpful reflection to start preparing your personalized plan.

2.  **Propose Your Triage Rubric:** This is your decision-making guide. For each incoming email, you'll ask: "What category does this belong to, and what action should I take?"
    *   **Action Required (Sensitive):** These are emails demanding significant decisions or changes (e.g., "Approve budget," "Grant system access").
        *   **Action:** **ALWAYS require your explicit human review and approval.** This is a critical safety step to prevent accidental or automated sensitive actions.
    *   **Action Required (Routine):** Emails needing a quick, straightforward response or click (e.g., "Confirm meeting," "Fill out short survey").
        *   **Action:** Review quickly, act, then archive.
    *   **Information Only:** Emails that you need to read but don't require a response (e.g., "Team update," "Newsletter").
        *   **Action:** Read, then archive.
    *   **Review Later:** Emails that are important but not urgent, or require focused time (e.g., "Long report," "Project research").
        *   **Action:** Move to a holding area for dedicated review.

3.  **Propose Your Folder/Label Structure:** Now, let's create homes for these categories. Think about how these will help you quickly identify and retrieve emails.
    *   For **Action Required (Sensitive)**: Consider a prominent folder/label like `_Requires-My-Approval` or `_Sensitive-Action-Needed`. The underscore often helps them sort to the top. This clearly flags items needing your personal attention.
    *   For **Action Required (Routine)**: A `_To-Do-Quick` folder or label can work well.
    *   For **Information Only**: A general `Archive/Info` folder, or specific folders like `Archive/Newsletters`, `Archive/Notifications`.
    *   For **Review Later**: A `_Later` or `_Read-When-Ready` folder.

By setting up these categories and dedicated spaces, you're building a powerful safety net that ensures critical tasks get your personal attention while routine items are handled efficiently.
