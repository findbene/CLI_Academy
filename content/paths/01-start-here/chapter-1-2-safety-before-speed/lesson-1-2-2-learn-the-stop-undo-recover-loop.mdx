# Learn the stop, undo, recover loop

## Objective

Sometimes, despite our best efforts, an AI agent builds something totally broken or gets stuck in an infinite loop. When that happens, your smartest move is to hit the brakes. 

We don't panic. We stop, undo, and recover.

## Walkthrough

### 1. Stop
If an AI agent is printing massive amounts of text and seems to be out of control, click on your terminal window and press **`Ctrl` + `C`** on your keyboard (even on a Mac). This is the universal "Kill Command." It will instantly stop whatever your terminal is doing.

### 2. Undo
If the AI wrote terrible code in your project, the fastest way back to safety is using `git`. If you use Git, simply typing `git restore .` will wipe away all the AI's uncommitted broken changes and magically restore your files to how they looked this morning.

### 3. Recover
If things get permanently stuck, open the Floating Tutor in the bottom right and say:
> *"I accidentally ran a bad command and I'm stuck. How do I recover?"*

> Keep this lesson open. If you ever feel overwhelmed in the future, scroll back to this page and remember that `Ctrl+C` is your emergency brake!
